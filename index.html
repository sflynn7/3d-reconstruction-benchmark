Benchmarking State-of-the-Art
3D Reconstruction Methods

A quantitative evaluation of four SOTA SfM and monocular depth methods on indoor iPhone-captured scenes, measured via ICP-aligned Cloud-to-Cloud distance metrics against stereo ground truth.

Structure from Motion VGGT Pi3 DepthAnything3 COLMAP
Methods evaluated

VGGT

ai-based

monocular depth

classical
COLMAP
SIFT-based SfM pipeline with MVS densification. The established classical baseline.
Dataset & pipeline

Indoor iPhone scenes

Three objects filmed with an iPhone 15 Pro under day and night lighting (~90 frames/scene at 3 fps). Ground truth from a ORBBEC Gemini 335Lg stereo camera (.BAG → PLY).

Ukulele Day Ukulele Night Artificial Plant Day Artificial Plant Night Electric Fan Day Electric Fan Night Lab Chair Mannequin Head

Evaluation pipeline

Scale reconstruction → manual ICP alignment → Cloud/Mesh Distance (Chamfer + Hausdorff) in CloudCompare. F-Score computed via custom Python script.

Key findings
01
AI-based methods (VGGT, Pi3) reconstruct dense geometry without explicit camera calibration, trading classical robustness for significant speed gains.
02
COLMAP failed sparse reconstruction on the Mannequin Head scene — insufficient SIFT features on the low-texture surface. Treated as a qualitative finding.
03
Lighting conditions (day vs. night) had a measurable impact on reconstruction quality across all methods tested.
